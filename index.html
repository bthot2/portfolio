# Publications

My research has been published in top-tier conferences and journals in the fields of Social Networks, and computer science. Below are some of my key publications.

Assessing the impact of social network on the formation and evolution of collective opinion in integrated intelligent argumentation with social network

Authors: Bhargav Krishna Thota; Xiaoqing Frank Liu

Journal: Social Network Analysis and Mining 15, 44 (2025)

Three novel measures directional impact, mutual impact, and social group impact were proposed to quantify the impact of social networking on cyber-argumentation. These different measures help quantify what the social group of a person thinks about his or her opinions and their impacts on their arguments. Using these impact measures, we developed an algorithm to detect the pseudo-supporters of an individual in an argumentation network. Furthermore, we also study the impact of the social network of users on significant shifts in collective opinion using the social group impact and the strength of the social connection of users in argumentation.

DOI

Assessing Impact of Social Network on Formation of Opinions in Integrated Intelligent Argumentation with Social Network

Authors: Bhargav Krishna Thota; Xiaoqing Frank Liu

Conference: 2023 IEEE/ACM International Conference on Advances in Social Networks Analysis and Mining (ASONAM '23)

Three types of measures: directional impact, mutual impact, and social group impact were developed to quantify the impact of social networks on argumentation. These different measures help quantify what a person's social group thinks about his or her opinions and their impacts on their arguments. Finally, using these impact measures, we developed an algorithm to detect the pseudo-supporters of an individual in an argumentation network.

Toward Mobile Application of Cyber Argumentation with Social Networking

Authors: Bhargav Krishna Thota; Xiaoqing Frank Liu; Md Mahfuzer Rahman

Conference: 2021 IEEE International Conference on Services Computing (SCC)

A prototype of an innovative mobile application of cyber argumentation with social networking and introduced multiple analytical capabilities of argumentation with social networking.
